# Villager Enchanting

Settlements villagers can enchant your gear — but unlike a regular enchanting table, the
result depends on the **villager's expertise**, their **intelligence**, and the
**enchanting setup**. A Master villager with a full library
produces dramatically better results than a Novice working with a bare enchanting table.

---

## How It Works

1. Place an **enchanting table** optionally surrounded by **bookshelves** (up to 15, just like
   vanilla).
2. Give an **unenchanted item** to a villager with access to the table.
3. The villager evaluates the item and applies enchantments automatically.
4. The enchanted item is returned to the villager's inventory, ready for you to collect.

The villager decides which enchantments to apply, how many, and at what level — all
based on their personal attributes and their environment. No lapis required.

---

## What Matters

Three things determine the quality of a villager enchanting session:

### Villager Expertise

Every villager has an expertise tier, from **Novice** to **Master**. Higher-tier
villagers have more enchanting power and can apply more enchantments in a single session.
Expertise is the single biggest factor in enchantment quality.

### Intelligence

Intelligence is a genetic trait that varies between villagers. A smarter villager
extracts more value from their expertise tier, producing slightly stronger results
overall. You can selectively breed villagers to improve intelligence over generations.

### Bookshelves

Bookshelves around the enchanting table provide a flat power bonus, up to a maximum of
**15 bookshelves**. More shelves means more enchanting power for every villager, regardless
of tier. A full bookshelf setup is always worth building.

---

## The One-Shot Rule

Once a villager enchants an item, **no villager can ever enchant that item again**.

This is permanent. Even if you strip all the enchantments off with a Grindstone, the
item remembers that it was villager-enchanted. It cannot re-enter the villager enchanting
system.

This means every enchanting session is a commitment. Choose your villager and setup carefully,
and don't waste your best gear on an inexperienced villager.

---

## Expertise Tiers at a Glance

| Tier       | Max Enchantments | Enchanting Power Level |
|------------|------------------|------------------------|
| Novice     | 1                | Low                    |
| Apprentice | 1                | Moderate               |
| Journeyman | 2                | Good                   |
| Expert     | 2                | High                   |
| Master     | 3                | Very High              |

Higher tiers unlock access to rarer and more expensive enchantments. Some enchantments
(like **Mending** or **Soul Speed**) require a minimum tier before any villager will
even attempt them.

---

## Examples

### Apprentice Villager — Iron Pickaxe

> **Setup:** Apprentice villager, average intelligence, 6 bookshelves.

The villager has a modest power budget and only gets **1 roll**. With limited power,
only affordable enchantments make the pool — things like Efficiency or Unbreaking at
low levels. The result might be:

- **Efficiency II**

A single, practical enchantment. Useful for early-game tools, but nothing spectacular.
The item is now permanently marked — no other villager will touch it.

### Master Villager — Diamond Sword

> **Setup:** Master villager, above-average intelligence, 15 bookshelves.

The villager has a massive power budget and gets up to **3 rolls**. Expensive enchantments
like Mending and Looting are now affordable, and the system favors higher levels. A
strong session might look like:

- **Mending**
- **Sharpness IV**
- **Looting II**

Three quality enchantments, anchored by Mending — the kind of result that makes a
Master villager worth protecting. The sword is done; it will never be villager-enchanted
again, but it's already endgame-ready.

---

## Tips & Strategy

- **Save your best items for your best villagers.** A Master session on a diamond sword
  is worth far more than a Novice session on the same item — and you only get one shot.
- **Build a full 15-bookshelf setup.** The power bonus from bookshelves applies equally
  to every tier. Even a Novice benefits from a proper library.
- **Breed for Intelligence.** Intelligence is genetic. Selectively breeding smart
  villagers over time will steadily improve your enchanting output.
- **Don't enchant end-game gear with novices.** The one-shot rule means you can't
  re-enchant an item later with a better villager. Wait until you have the villager to match.
